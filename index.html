Nate Garrelts

ASC 3080 · Ferris State University · Big Rapids, MI 49307 · (616) 591-5899 · nategarrelts@ferris.edu

I am a Professor of English at Ferris State University where I teach technical writing, business writing, and advanced composition. In addition to publishing on topics ranging from instructional comics to film representations of pimps, I have edited four collections on digital games. My first book, Digital Gameplay (2005), examined the intersections between games and gamers. Later books help readers understand a single game or franchise by presenting multiple academic perspectives. These books include The Meaning and Culture of Grand Theft Auto (2006), Understanding Minecraft (2014), and Responding to Call of Duty (2017). Prior to coming to Ferris I served as a writing center director, writing program coordinator, and primary investigator on several grants. My current research uses machine learning and natural language processing to better understand social media, literature, and other cultural artifacts. Although I have experience with many languages, libraries, frameworks, and databases, I prefer JavaScript, Node.js, jQuery, Cheerio, Bootstrap, and MongoDB.
